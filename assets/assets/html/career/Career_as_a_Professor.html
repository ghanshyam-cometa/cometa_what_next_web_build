Career as a Professor

Definition of Professor

A Professor is a professional who teaches the students at the University or Institute level in a specific stream of education. A professor is generally specialised in one specific subject which he teaches in University. The professors with adequate knowledge and experience in the field can also go for research in the respective fields and write research papers. A Professor can pursue his career in a private or government-based University/Institutes. They can also go for research-based institutes as well as coaching centres.

Eligibility

The eligibility criteria which must be fulfilled to become a Professor is given below:

  1. The candidate must have passed 10+2 in any stream from a recognised board.

  2. The candidate must have passed a graduation/ postgraduation level degree in any stream from a recognized institute.

  3. The candidates can also go for a PhD degree in the preferred stream to avail a career path in Research-based institutions.

  4. Qualifying UGC NET is necessary if you want to take this profession.

Type of Job Roles Professor

The different job roles for a Professor are given below: -

Professor: A professor is a person who is responsible for teaching the students at the University or Institute based on their knowledge level and expertise in the preferred stream.

Researcher: A researcher is a person who is responsible to research in a specific subject of specialization and write research papers on the findings.

Assistant Professor: An Assistant Professor is a professional who is responsible for assisting the professor and overviewing the different aspects of teaching such as preparing lessons, managing courses, lecturing, and facilitating learning.

HOD: An HOD is the Head of Department who ensures the proper management and leading of a particular educational department.

Associate Professor: An Associate Professor is a person who is responsible for various teaching activities such as teaching, conducting research, attending conferences, solving administrative issues and supervising students.
